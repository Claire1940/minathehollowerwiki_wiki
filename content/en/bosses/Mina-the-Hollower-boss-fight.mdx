<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Combat Mechanics for Boss Encounters">

To succeed in any **Mina the Hollower boss fight**, players must look past the charming 8-bit visuals and master the technical depth of Mina's movement. Unlike traditional action-adventures, combat here is vertical and horizontal, utilizing the ground itself as a defensive tool. The "Hollower" skillset allows Mina to submerge, granting temporary invulnerability and repositioning opportunities that are essential for late-game survival.

| Mechanic | Input Action | Boss Fight Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Burrow** | Jump then submerge | Dodge charging attacks/projectiles |
| **Nightstar Whip** | Primary Attack | Mid-range poking and safe spacing |
| **Sidearm Use** | Secondary Tool | Burst damage or crowd control |
| **Vaulting** | Surface from burrow | Closing gaps or jumping over low sweeps |
| **Weapon Swap** | Inventory/Quick Menu | Adapting to boss resistance/speed |

<Callout type="warning" title="Burrow Timing Warning">
The burrow animation has a slight startup delay. If you wait until a boss is already mid-charge to submerge, you will likely take damage. Preemptive burrowing is the only reliable way to avoid fast-moving threats like the charging enemies found in the early dungeons.
</Callout>

Movement in the game features a slight acceleration curve. While some players describe it as "controlling a boat," this momentum is designed to reward committed movement. In a boss arena, standing still is a death sentence. You must maintain constant motion to keep the boss from pinning you against the screen boundaries.

<Section icon={Target} title="Early Game Boss Walkthroughs">

The initial hurdles in the game serve as a "skill check" for your mastery of burrowing and spacing. Two major encounters define the early experience: the **Gator Boss** and the **Big Thug** sub-boss. These fights teach you that while tanking damage is possible, surgical precision is the intended path to victory.

### The Gator Boss Strategy
The Gator Boss utilizes heavy physical sweeps and a snapping lunge. Its massive size makes it an easy target for the Nightstar whip, but its reach is equally impressive.

| Phase | Boss Behavior | Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | Slow tail sweeps | Jump or burrow under the tail arc |
| **Phase 2** | Rapid snapping lunges | Burrow and move perpendicular to the lunge |
| **Phase 3** | Ground slams/Debris | Stay submerged until the dust settles |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Engagement">
    Use the Nightstar whip to land 2-3 hits from maximum range. Do not over-extend, as the Gator Boss counters quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Managing the Lunge">
    When the Gator pulls its head back, immediately burrow. Do not try to outrun the lunge on the surface.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sidearm Burst">
    During the cooldown after a ground slam, use your sidearms (like the Boomerang Sword) to deal massive burst damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="The Big Thug Shortcut">
The **Big Thug** sub-boss is notoriously difficult to dodge due to his wide swing arc. Some players find it more efficient to "tank" his hits while dealing maximum DPS with the whip, provided they have found at least one health upgrade. However, for a no-damage run, you must flee the moment his arm twitches.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Mid-Game Challenge: The Kraken">

The Kraken represents the first major environmental boss fight. Unlike the Gator, the Kraken limits your movement via water hazards and multiple tentacles. This fight is less about raw damage and more about arena management.

**Key Kraken Tactics:**
- **Tentacle Priority**: Always clear the active tentacles before focusing on the Kraken's head.
- **Burrowing on Bridges**: Be careful; some bridges are only one tile wide. If you try to burrow here, you may fall or fail to submerge, leaving you vulnerable to the Kraken's ink spray.
- **Telegraphed Sweeps**: The Kraken will raise a tentacle high before a sweep. This is your cue to burrow and wait for the "whoosh" sound effect before resurfacing.

<Callout type="info" title="Environmental Awareness">
The Kraken arena features destructible platforms. If you spend too much time in one spot, the Kraken will destroy the floor, forcing you into the water where Mina moves significantly slower and cannot burrow.
</Callout>

</Section>
