<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Hammer Combat Mechanics">

Success with the **Mina the Hollower hammer** hinges on understanding the rhythm of "Hollowing." Because the maul features long recovery frames, you cannot simply mash the attack button. Instead, you must weave burrowing actions between your heavy hits. Burrowing allows Mina to disappear under the floor, avoiding incoming projectiles and repositioning behind an enemy's shield.

The hammer's moveset is built around three core actions:
1. **Quick Strike**: A standard overhead smash for finishing off weakened foes.
2. **Charged Blast**: A massive explosion that consumes a portion of your commitment but deals heavy guard damage.
3. **Burrow Pop-up**: Entering a burrow and immediately attacking as you emerge provides a surprise hit with reduced wind-up time.

| Move Name | Input Command | Damage Tier | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Smash** | Tap Attack | Moderate | Clearing trash mobs |
| **Explosive Maul** | Hold Attack | **Massive** | Bosses and armored units |
| **Vaulting Strike** | Jump + Attack | High | Avoiding ground hazards |
| **Burrow Burst** | Attack from Burrow | Moderate | Repositioning / Counter-play |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Beware of lava or specialized "anti-burrow" tiles. In areas like the later Spark Towers, these hazards prevent you from using Hollowing to escape, making the hammer's slow speed a significant liability.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Bone Economy and Progression Strategy">

In *Mina the Hollower*, bones serve as both your gold and your experience points. For a hammer user, losing a large stockpile of bones is devastating because you need them to upgrade your Maul's damage output at the blacksmith in Ossex City. The bone economy scales as you progress, meaning enemies in deeper zones like South Bone Beach drop significantly higher amounts than those in the opening areas.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Bones">
    Defeat enemies and smash environmental objects. Larger enemies drop "Condensed Bones" which provide a higher value but are lost upon death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage Risk">
    If you are carrying a large amount of bones, look for a checkpoint or a "Spark Store." You can safely deposit a portion of your currency here to prevent losing it if you die.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reclaim Losses">
    If you fall in battle, a spirit orb remains at the site of your death. You have **one chance** to return and reclaim your lost bones. Dying a second time permanently deletes that stockpile.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Invest in Upgrades">
    Visit the Ossex City blacksmith. Prioritize the "Attack Power" upgrade for the hammer to ensure you can one-shot basic enemies, reducing the time you spend vulnerable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Economic Tip">
Use the "Bone Storm" sidearm if you find it. It allows you to throw bones as a projectile, giving the hammer a much-needed ranged option without sacrificing your primary weapon's power.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Boss Strategies for Hammer Users">

Boss fights in *Mina the Hollower* are often described as "Bloodborne-esque" in their intensity. As a hammer user, your goal is to find the "Tell"—the specific animation a boss performs before an attack—and use your charge attack to punish their recovery window.

The first major hurdle is the **Duchess**, a giant animated statue. Hammer users must resist the urge to trade hits. Instead, strafe her attacks, wait for her tentacles to emerge, and land a single charged hit on her core.

| Boss Name | Hammer Difficulty | Key Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Duchess** | Moderate | Strafe and punish tentacle slams |
| **Rock Guardian** | **Hard** | Use burrow to avoid boulder tosses |
| **Spark Sentinel** | Easy | Charge attack during his reload phase |
| **Ossex Traitor** | Moderate | Parry with the shield sidearm |

<Callout type="tip" title="Healing Strategy">
Remember that healing with Plasma takes roughly **3 seconds** and leaves you immobile. Only heal after a boss has finished a major combo or while you are safely burrowed (if the terrain allows).
</Callout>
