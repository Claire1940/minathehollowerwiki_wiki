<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Progression in the Demo">
Even in the limited scope of the demo, players get a solid understanding of the combat system and early progression. Mina can collect "bones" from defeated enemies, which act as currency and experience. These bones can be converted into "bone stones" for safe keeping or used to level up Mina's attributes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Weapon Selection">
    At the start, players choose their primary weapon from options like nimble daggers, a crushing mace (Nightstar), or a hammer with explosive force. Each offers a distinct playstyle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Plasma Vials and Healing">
    Plasma vials are crucial for healing. Players gain plasma by attacking enemies, then use vials to convert it into health. This creates a risk-reward dynamic in combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bone Collection and Upgrades">
    Defeated enemies drop bones. These can be used at specific locations to "bone up" (level up) Mina's attack, defense, or sidearm power. They can also be converted into bone stones to prevent loss upon death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Sidearms and Trinkets">
    The demo introduces various sidearms (like lobbing hatchets) and trinkets that offer exotic effects, allowing players to customize Mina's abilities and adapt to different combat scenarios.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The combat is challenging but fair; enemies hit hard, but controls are tight, ensuring that player skill is paramount. The grappling hook, while not always heavily featured in the very initial demo segments, is a key movement ability that promises dynamic traversal in the full game.

<Callout type="warning">
Be mindful of your bone count! Dying before converting bones to bone stones will result in their loss, making early progression slightly more challenging.
</Callout>
