<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering Hollowing and Movement Mechanics">

The core of your adventure on Tenebrous Isle revolves around **Mina the Hollower tips** regarding her signature move: hollowing. Unlike traditional top-down adventure games where jumping or crossing gaps requires specific items, Mina can burrow into the ground at will. This "hollowing" serves as your primary tool for speed, evasion, and puzzle-solving. When you burrow, you move significantly faster than your standard walking pace, but you must be mindful of the timing required to enter and exit the ground.

Hollowing provides a brief window of invulnerability, allowing you to dive under enemy projectiles or charging attacks. However, the animation for emerging from the ground leaves you momentarily vulnerable. This creates a high-stakes "micro-decision" loop where you must decide if the speed boost is worth the risk of being caught upon resurfacing.

<Callout type="tip" title="Burrowing Strategy">
If you encounter a blockade or a guard that refuses to move, your first instinct should always be to hollow. Many early-game "soft locks" reported by players are actually simple burrowing puzzles. Look for soft earth or crevices that indicate a path beneath the obstacle.

Beyond your primary weapon, the sidearm system adds a layer of tactical depth. These sub-weapons are dropped by destructible objects and can be swapped on the fly. Each sub-weapon has a "hidden" interaction that can be triggered by your primary attacks.

| Sub-Weapon | Primary Effect | Advanced Interaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

<Callout type="warning" title="Sidearm Retention">
Be aware that sidearms are lost upon death. Unlike your primary weapon and bones (which can be recovered), sidearms must be found again in the world. Save your powerful axes for boss encounters rather than wasting them on common mobs.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Bone-Up Progression System">

Progression in Mina the Hollower utilizes the "Bone-up" system, a unique blend of traditional RPG leveling and currency management. As you defeat enemies and hollow through the earth, you collect bones. These act as your experience points. Once you reach a certain threshold, you can level up one of your core attributes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Bones Accumulation">
    Defeat enemies and dig in soft spots to gather bones. Digging is often more lucrative than combat in the early regions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stat Selection">
    Choose between Attack, Defense, or Agility. Attack increases raw damage, while Defense reduces the "bone loss" penalty upon death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Trinket Investment">
    Save a portion of your bones to purchase trinkets from NPCs. These provide passive buffs like longer burrow time or increased health recovery.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Death Recovery">
    If you fall in battle, you leave behind a pile of bones. You must return to that spot to reclaim your progress, similar to Souls-like mechanics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stat Category | Benefit | Recommended Build |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Attack Power | Higher damage per swing | The "Glass Cannon" (Focus on Attack/Agility) |
| Defense | Reduced damage taken | The "Tank" (Focus on Defense/Health) |
| Agility | Faster hollowing and recovery | The "Speedster" (Focus on Agility/Trinkets) |

<Callout type="success" title="Health Management">
Remember that hitting enemies refills your health vial. If you are low on health, don't just run away; find a slower enemy and land a few hits to regain your sustain. This is the most critical of all **Mina the Hollower tips** for surviving long dungeons.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Early Boss Strategies: Mole and Thorne">

The first two boss encounters serve as skill checks for your understanding of hollowing and spacing. The first boss, a massive subterranean creature, requires you to use the environment to your advantage. Hide behind the indestructible rock formations when the boss prepares its charging attack. Wait for the recovery window after it hits a wall to unleash your sub-weapon.

For the second boss, Thorne, the difficulty spikes significantly. Thorne is a rival warrior who uses high-mobility spear attacks and fire tornadoes. The key here is patience.

| Boss Phase | Thorne's Attack | Counter Maneuver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Aerial Jump | Slams down from above | Wait for shadow, hollow away, then strike twice |
| Fire Tornado | Encirclement | Stay in the center or burrow under the flames |
| Spear Thrust | Long-range poke | Side-step and use a sub-weapon like the Axe |
| Desperation | Rapid fire waves | Focus on hollowing for invulnerability frames |

<Callout type="tip" title="Thorne Combat Window">
Thorne is only vulnerable for about 2 seconds after he lands from a jump. Do not get greedy. Land two hits with your whip or mace and immediately hollow away to avoid his counter-swing.
</Callout>

</Section>
