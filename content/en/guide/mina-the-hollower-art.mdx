<Section icon={Shield} title="Art Direction and Gameplay Clarity">
While the retro aesthetic of **Mina the Hollower art** is undeniably gorgeous, it also occasionally presents challenges for gameplay clarity. The moody, atmospheric texture, while beautiful, can sometimes make it difficult to distinguish between interactable elements and environmental dressing. Players might find themselves guessing whether a tile, ledge, or prop is a pathway or a hazard.

This visual ambiguity is most noticeable in dimly lit areas or sections with heavy use of similar color shades. Shadows and decorative elements can share the same visual weight as actual hazards, disrupting the flow of exploration. However, this is a minor friction point that doesn't detract significantly from the overall experience. The game trusts players to learn its visual language, even if it requires a bit of trial and error initially.
