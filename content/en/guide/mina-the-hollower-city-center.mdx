Arriving at the **mina the hollower city center**, known as Ossex, is the first major milestone in your journey across Tenebrous Isle. <Section icon={Swords} title="Economy and Essential Early Upgrades">

Bones are the lifeblood of your progression in the **mina the hollower city center**. While standard Bones are lost upon death, **Bonestones** are permanent currency gems that stay in your inventory regardless of how many times you fall in battle. Your first hour in Ossex should be dedicated to gathering these gems to fund your initial power spikes.

One of the most critical upgrades available in the early game is the Spark system. By visiting the Emporium, you can purchase a Spark upgrade. Having two Sparks allows you to open a specific door to the left of the pawn shop, which leads to the Iron Steed bike. This vehicle is not just for speed; it allows Mina to jump across gaps that are three squares wide, opening up entirely new exploration routes.

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Penalty">
If you die without a Spark, you lose a significant portion of your carried Bones. Investing in the **Bell of Grace** trinket from Frayd in the Station Underside can mitigate this risk by giving you a chance to retain your Spark or your currency.
</Callout>

| Item | Location | Cost | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Kear (Key) | Bird Shop | 300+ Bones | Opens locked gates/chests |
| Spark Upgrade | Emporium | Variable | Extra life & Door access |
| Iron Steed | 2-Spark Door | Free (Find) | Crosses 3-tile gaps |
| Axe Subweapon | Strategy Center | Free (Find) | Ranged combat & Puzzles |
| Bonestone Dust | Poppit's Shop | Variable | Permanent currency |

Beyond the shops, you can find "free" riches by interacting with the environment. For instance, looking through the telescope north of the Ossex checkpoint and spotting a specific planet will cause a Kear to fall from the sky. Similarly, using the Axe subweapon to break a chest in the Strategy Center provides an easy early-game boost.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Hollower's Guild: Unlocking the World Map">

The Hollower's Guild is arguably the most important building in the **mina the hollower city center**, yet it starts the game boarded up and inaccessible. Restoring the Guild is a multi-step process that rewards you with the most essential tool for any explorer: the World Map. Without the map, navigating the sprawling Tenebrous Isle becomes a daunting task of trial and error.

To begin the restoration, you must find the side entrance to the building. Follow the exterior wall to the right, hop over the rocks, and clear the crates to find the back door. Once inside, you will meet Muriel, who sets the rescue mission in motion.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Rebel Barracks">
    Head directly south from the main entrance of Ossex. You will need a Kear to unlock the gate leading to the barracks where the Guild members are being held.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Rescue Rhene">
    Fight through the barracks and locate Rhene. You must escort her safely back to the Guild hall. She is a fellow Hollower who helps manage the facility.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Free Drillhardt">
    Once Rhene is back, return to the Guild to find Drillhardt. He is stuck in the floor—burrow directly under his antlers to pop him out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Purchase the Map">
    Talk to Drillhardt in the newly cleaned Guild hall. He will offer the **Map** for 500 Bones. This is a mandatory purchase for efficient progression.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Guild Benefits">
Once the Guild is active, Drillhardt provides various Underlab upgrades. Additionally, keep an eye on the newspapers in the hall; they provide hints about the "intended" route and the location of the next recommended dungeon.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Must-Have Trinkets Near the City Center">

Trinkets function similarly to charms in other action-RPGs, providing passive buffs that can be swapped at checkpoints. Before venturing into the dangerous eastern swamps or western volcanic regions, you should collect a baseline set of Trinkets found within or immediately adjacent to the **mina the hollower city center**.

The **Steady Soles** are an absolute priority. These allow Mina to move through water, grass, and snow without the standard speed penalty. They are hidden in an attic residence near the eastern exit of Ossex. You must reach the balcony overlooking the courtyard and burrow under a specific opening to access the treasure room.

Instead of finishing him off, pay 1,000 Bones to "restore" him. It is a steep price for the early game, but the safety net it provides for your Bone collection is invaluable.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Combat Preparation and First Dungeon Steps">

Before leaving the **mina the hollower city center** to tackle your first generator, you should spend time grinding for levels. The game's difficulty curve is steepest at the beginning when your health is low. A prime farming spot is located just east of Ossex in **Mourner's Mile**. Red demons jump out of crypts here, yielding 25 Bones each. With a nearby save point, you can clear the screen, rest, and repeat to gain several levels in minutes.

The recommended first dungeon is the **Queensbury Crypt**, located to the east. It is designed to teach you the advanced nuances of the burrowing mechanic. Ensure you have at least two Kears and the Steady Soles before entering, as the environment can be just as deadly as the enemies.

<Callout type="tip" title="Final Preparation">
Don't hoard your Bones! Every time you return to the **mina the hollower city center**, spend your currency on Attack or Defense levels. It is better to have a permanent stat boost than to lose a pile of Bones to a surprise trap in the wild.
</Callout>
