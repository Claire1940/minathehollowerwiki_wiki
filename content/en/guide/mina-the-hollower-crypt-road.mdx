<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Systems and Weapon Selection">

Combat in Mina the Hollower is deliberate and tactical. Drawing inspiration from classic titles like Castlevania, the movement and attack animations require precise timing. On the **mina the hollower crypt road**, you will face a variety of enemies that require different approaches depending on your equipped armament. At the start of your journey, you are presented with a choice of weapons that will define your early-game playstyle.

| Weapon Name | Range | Speed | Special Characteristic |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Nightstar Whip** | Medium | Moderate | Classic versatile reach, good for keeping distance |
| **Whisper & Vesper** | Short | Very Fast | Dual daggers for high DPS but requires proximity |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Hollowing Mechanics and Movement Strategy">

The defining feature of **mina the hollower crypt road** is the hollowing (burrowing) mechanic. This was developed to solve the "shadow problem" inherent in top-down perspectives. In many games, jumping makes it difficult to judge exactly where your character will land. Mina solves this by keeping the character's "hitbox" on the ground level even when evading.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Burrow">
    Press the burrow button to dive into the earth. While underground, Mina moves faster and is immune to most high-level projectiles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Navigate Hazards">
    Use burrowing to pass under fences, low gates, and certain enemy projectiles like the Fire Crow's lingering flames.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Pop-Out Attack">
    Resurfacing directly under or next to an enemy can sometimes provide a tactical opening, though it requires precise positioning.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Chained Movement">
    Combine burrowing with standard movement to create a rhythm that keeps Mina out of the line of fire while closing the gap on ranged foes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Movement Action | Advantage | Limitation |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Standard Walk** | Full combat readiness | Slowest movement speed |
| **Burrowing** | High speed, projectile immunity | Cannot attack while underground |
| **Bouncing** | Clear small gaps, reposition | Vulnerable to air-to-ground hits |

<Callout type="success" title="Evasion Mastery">
When a Torch Guy throws a projectile, don't try to jump over it. Burrowing allows you to stay exactly where your shadow is, maintaining a 1:1 relationship with the ground and making evasion 100% more predictable.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression Guide: Bones and Leveling Up">

The economy of the **mina the hollower crypt road** revolves around **Bones**. This currency is used for both character progression and purchasing world-altering items. Unlike traditional linear platformers, Mina features RPG elements that allow you to customize your stats to suit your preferred weapon.

| Stat Category | Effect on Gameplay | Recommended For |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Power** | Increases raw damage of primary weapons | Maul and Dagger users |
| **Defense** | Reduces damage taken from all sources | New players / Melee builds |
| **Subweapon** | Increases damage and efficiency of sidearms | Tactical/Ranged playstyles |
| **Utility** | Improves burrow speed and resource gain | Completionists and speedrunners |

As you explore Ossex and the surrounding outskirts, you will find shops that trade Bones for Trinkets and Keys. Trinkets provide passive bonuses—such as increased health or faster attack speeds—while Keys are necessary to unlock new sectors of the Crypt Road and the Queensbury Crypts.
