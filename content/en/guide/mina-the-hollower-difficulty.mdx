<Section icon={Target} title="The Spark System and Risk Management">

The core of **Mina the Hollower difficulty** lies in the Spark system. This mechanic governs how you lose and retain progress. Unlike traditional RPGs where death might simply mean a reload, Mina uses a system reminiscent of "Souls-like" games but with a unique twist involving "Bones"—which serve as both currency and experience points.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Spark">
    A turquoise dot next to your health. Losing it makes you vulnerable to losing all held Bones upon your next death.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bone Loss">
    If you die without a Spark, your Bones are dropped. You must return to the death site and defeat the enemy that claimed them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recovery">
    Leveling up (Boning Up) or purchasing a new Spark at a checkpoint restores your protection immediately.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

This system creates a constant tension: do you push forward to the next checkpoint with high Bone counts, or do you retreat to spend them and "bank" your progress? The difficulty is further modulated by how many Sparks you can carry, with a maximum capacity of five available later in the game.

| Death Penalty | Condition | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minor Death** | Spark is active | Lose Spark, retain all Bones and items |
| **Major Death** | No Spark active | Lose all current Bones; must retrieve them |
| **Tool Loss** | Any death | Sub-weapons/sidearms are dropped at death site |
| **Trinket Status** | Any death | Permanent accessories are never lost |

<Callout type="warning" title="Retaining Your Tools">
Sidearms like the Axe or Javelin are powerful but fragile. If you die, they remain at your grave. If you die again before reaching them, those specific tool instances may be lost forever.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mastery: Weapons and Burrowing">

Success in Mina the Hollower requires mastering the "Burrow" and choosing the right weapon for your playstyle. Combat is deliberate; you cannot simply mash buttons and expect to survive. Every swing has a recovery period, and every enemy has a specific tell that you must exploit.

**Weapon Archetypes:**
- **Whip:** The "normal" choice with balanced range and speed.
- **Flash Strike Maul:** Extremely slow wind-up but massive damage and knockback.
- **Daggers:** High speed and low range, requiring a "glass cannon" approach.
- **Sidearms:** Consumable projectiles (Axes, Javelins) that provide ranged options.

The Burrow mechanic is Mina's signature move. By holding the action button, Mina dives underground. This makes her invulnerable to most projectiles and ground-based attacks. Emerging from a burrow provides a small speed boost and can be transitioned directly into a jump-attack.

| Weapon Type | Speed | Range | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maul** | Very Slow | Medium | Bosses with long recovery windows |
| **Whip** | Medium | High | General exploration and crowd control |
| **Daggers** | Fast | Short | High-mobility players and speedruns |
| **Axe (Sidearm)** | Slow | Arc | Overcoming shields or vertical foes |

<Callout type="success" title="The Sludgy Health Strategy">
Equipping the 'Sludgy Health Vial' trinket changes your damage taken into a slow-draining bar. If you heal before the bar hits zero, you effectively negate the damage, allowing you to 'tank' hits that would otherwise be fatal.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Boning Up: Progression Strategy">

The leveling system, known as "Boning Up," is inspired by Zelda II. When you collect enough Bones, you are forced to make a choice. You cannot hoard experience indefinitely; you must invest in your character's growth to survive the escalating **Mina the Hollower difficulty**.

Trinkets are the other half of progression. With over 60 trinkets in the game, you can create "builds." For example, combining a mobility trinket with a projectile-reflecting trinket allows you to play aggressively against ranged enemies. Trinkets are permanent and provide the most significant boosts to Mina's capabilities.

| Stat Category | Benefit | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack** | Higher damage per swing | Shortens boss fights significantly |
| **Defense** | Reduced damage taken | Increases room for error in dungeons |
| **Sidearm** | More damage/utility for tools | Crucial for players who rely on range |
| **Bone Stone** | Secure currency storage | Essential for late-game weapon upgrades |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pawn Shop Tactics">
If you find the game too easy, you can visit the Pawn Shop to sell your initial health, sparks, or even your vials. Finishing the game with a "negative completion percentage" is a high-level achievement for the most dedicated players.
