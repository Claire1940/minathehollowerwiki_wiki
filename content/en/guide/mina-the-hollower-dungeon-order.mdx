<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the Mina the Hollower Dungeon Order">
Upon reaching Ossex in *Mina the Hollower*, players are tasked with repairing six generators across Tenebrous Isle. While the game offers significant freedom, allowing you to tackle the first four generators in almost any order, a structured approach can greatly enhance your early game experience. This guide outlines a recommended **Mina the Hollower dungeon order** that prioritizes easier routes and crucial early-game upgrades.

The open-world nature of *Mina the Hollower*, inspired by classics like *Link's Awakening*, means you have most tools available from the start. However, some dungeons may require a deeper understanding of Mina's burrowing abilities or offer more challenging encounters, making a strategic approach beneficial.

**Video Highlights:**
- The first four generators can be completed in any order.
- The game recommends the northeast dungeon (Queensbury Crypt) first.
- Obtaining the Proto Spark from the northeast dungeon provides a free revive.
- The primed vial pouch is another invaluable early-game trinket.
- Investing Bones into Attack and Defense is crucial for early progression.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Preparing for Your First Dungeon">
Before you embark on your first generator run, especially towards Queensbury Crypt, it's crucial to ensure Mina is adequately prepared. Proper preparation can significantly reduce frustration and bone loss.

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't Rush In Unprepared">
Entering a new area without sufficient upgrades or an understanding of the path can lead to frequent deaths and loss of valuable Bones. Always return to Ossex to regroup and upgrade if a route feels too difficult.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire the Isle Map">
    As mentioned, buy the **Isle Map** from Drillhardt in the Hollower's Guild. This is the single most important step for navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend or Protect Bones">
    Before heading out, either spend your collected Bones on upgrades or convert them into **Bone Stones** at the Guild to prevent losing them upon death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Invest in Attack and Defense">
    Use your Bones to increase Mina's **Attack** power and **Defense** stat. These direct upgrades make combat much more manageable in the early game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Ensure Adequate Healing">
    Stock up on healing vials. Consider acquiring the **Primed Vial Pouch** trinket (found in a house south of Ossex, entered via chimney) to carry two extra vials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Check the Tenebrous Times">
    Read the in-game newspaper. It often provides subtle hints and guidance on where to go next, reacting to your progress (or lack thereof).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Importance of Proto Spark in Queensbury Crypt">
One of the primary reasons to prioritize Queensbury Crypt as your first dungeon is the opportunity to acquire the **Proto Spark** trinket. This item provides a free revive upon death, making challenging encounters and boss fights significantly more forgiving.

To obtain the Proto Spark:
1.  In Queensbury Crypt, reach the red-carpet room just before the Ancestral Chamber.
2.  Take the **left path** from this room.
3.  Spend the required vials on the side route to proceed.
4.  Find **the Duke** and assist him with his escort quest after defeating the boss in that area.
5.  The Duke will reward you with the Proto Spark.

| Trinket      | Effect                                       | Location              | Early Game Value                                                      |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Proto Spark** | Grants one free revive upon death.           | Queensbury Crypt (Duke) | Extremely high; makes bosses and difficult rooms much less punishing.  |
| **Primed Vial Pouch** | Carry two additional healing vials.      | House south of Ossex  | High; increases survivability by extending healing capacity.           |
| **Bell of Grace** | Chance to retain Spark; keeps half Bones on Sparkless demise. | Ossex (Thief's hideout) | Moderate; good for mitigating bone loss, but secondary to Proto Spark. |

<Callout type="success" title="Don't Skip the Duke's Quest">
While the main path through Queensbury Crypt might seem straightforward, actively seeking out the Duke's side quest for the Proto Spark is a game-changer. This trinket will be invaluable for your continued journey through Tenebrous Isle.
</Callout>
