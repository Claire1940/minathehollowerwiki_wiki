Trinkets in Mina the Hollower function similarly to charms in Hollow Knight, providing significant buffs and unique abilities. Equipping the right trinkets can dramatically change your gameplay experience, offering enhanced survivability, increased damage, or improved exploration capabilities.

### Early Game Must-Have Trinkets

| Trinket Name | Effect | Acquisition Location | Key Benefit |
| :----------- | :----- | :------------------- | :---------- |
| **Proto Spark** | Resurrects Mina with partial health upon death; recharges at checkpoints. | Escort the Duke near the end of Queensbury Crypt. | Acts as an extra life, crucial for boss fights. |
| **Steady Soles** | No speed penalty on various surfaces; resists knockback. | Attic of a residence, left of the eastern Ossex checkpoint stairs. | Essential for specific dungeons (e.g., Bayou) and general mobility. |
| **Chain Capacitor** | Boosts damage for consecutive hits. | Building north of eastern Ossex exit (requires Kear for entry), complete car minigame. | Great for aggressive playstyles and melting bosses. |
| **Bell of Grace** | Chance to retain Spark on death, reduces bone loss. | Frayd in Station Underside (west Ossex), after paying 1,000 bones. | Mitigates death penalties, especially useful early on. |
| **Plasma Funnel** | Doubles vial drinking speed; prevents vial loss if interrupted. | Trinket Bazaar (west of Ossex, past Molten Foundry). | Improves healing reliability during intense combat. |

### How to Acquire the Proto Spark

<Section icon={Target} title="Currency and Upgrades: Making the Most of Your Bones">
In Mina the Hollower, **Bones** are your primary currency, used for leveling up, purchasing items, and acquiring crucial upgrades. **Bonestones** are a special form of currency that persists even after death, making them incredibly valuable for early game progression.

| Currency Type | Description | Primary Use | Persistence on Death |
| :------------ | :---------- | :---------- | :------------------- |
| **Bones** | Standard currency dropped by enemies. | Leveling up stats (Attack, Armor, Sidearm), buying items. | Lost on death if no Spark is active, or if you die again before retrieving. |
| **Bonestones** | Rare, persistent currency found in chests. | Used for significant purchases and upgrades that require larger sums. | **Persists** through death. |
| **Bone Dust** | Stored bones, safe from loss on death. | Can be converted from bones at checkpoints, or found. | **Persists** through death. |
| **Kears (Keys)** | Special keys for locked areas. | Unlocking doors, shortcuts, and accessing hidden content. | Must be purchased or found. |

<Callout type="tip">
Don't be a hoarder! Spend your bones on impactful upgrades like the Spark upgrade and essential trinkets. Early investments yield greater returns, making subsequent bone farming more efficient.
</Callout>

### Early Bone Farming Strategy

To quickly accumulate bones for early upgrades, consider this farming spot:

1.  **Head East of Ossex**: Go to Mourner's Mile, on the way to the Queensbury Crypt.
2.  **Locate Crypts**: Find the area where red demons continuously jump out of crypts.
3.  **Equip Axe Sidearm**: The axe is a great ranged weapon for this area. You can find one near a candle on the bridge before the graveyard entrance.
4.  **Farm Demons**: Kill the red demons (each gives 25 bones).
5.  **Reset at Checkpoint**: Return to the checkpoint one screen to the left to respawn enemies. Repeat.

This method allows you to gain several hundred bones per minute, significantly boosting your early game progression.
