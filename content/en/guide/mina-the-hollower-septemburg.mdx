<Section icon={Map} title="Septemburg Overview and Initial Exploration">
Septemburg is a crucial region in Mina the Hollower, functioning as a sprawling dungeon filled with unique challenges, enemies, and secrets. This agricultural region is locked in perpetual autumn, with crops merging with wildlife to create aggressive monstrosities. Expect howling winds and discordant lightning as you guide Mina through this harvest-themed area.

Upon entering Septemburg, you'll find an Underlab for saving your progress. From there, head left to encounter a Crow warning you about the "Corvidden" and Crow Town. Proceeding further, you'll face "Red Panda" enemies that hop around and are challenging to hit. These enemies have relatively low health, making them manageable with quick attacks. Utilize "Air Paddles" to traverse gaps and reach hidden areas, often containing Bones. A new enemy, the "Pumpkin Shooter," emerges from "Light Pumpkins," requiring multiple whips to defeat and collect all bones.

<Callout type="tip" title="Hidden Paths and Collectibles">
Always be on the lookout for hidden air paddles or destructible stumps. These often lead to secret screens with valuable Bonestone chests or other collectibles. For instance, destroying a stump to the far south after the Pumpkin Shooters can reveal a hidden path with rotating air paddles, leading to a chest containing **500 Bonestone**.
</Callout>

As you advance, you'll encounter various Crow Enemies: pitchfork-wielding crows that stab like spears and torch crows that shoot fire. Remember that you can burrow under sickles and pitchforks to pick them up and throw them back at enemies, a highly effective combat strategy.

|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Red Panda** | Hops erratically, low health | Quick whip attacks | Bones |
| **Pumpkin Shooter** | Emerges from "Light Pumpkins" | Multiple whip strikes (whip, whip, jump whip) | Bones |
| **Pitchfork Crow** | Stabs with pitchfork | Burrow under attacks, throw pitchforks | Pebbles |
| **Torch Crow** | Shoots fire | Use thrown sickles, rapid attacks | Vial |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Septemburg: The Relentless Chase and Kid Rescue">
A significant portion of Septemburg involves a thrilling chase sequence with the **Carving Man**, an invulnerable entity that will relentlessly pursue you. During this chase, your primary objective (besides survival) is to rescue three lost school kids, which will reward you with a valuable Trinket. Missing a kid means you'll have to purchase the Trinket from the black market later for a hefty sum of Bone.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Carving Man: No Fighting">
Do not attempt to fight the Carving Man during the chase sequences. He is completely invulnerable. Focus solely on evasion and objectives. Using sub-weapons like the Recall Disc can aid in escaping tight situations by warping past him.
</Callout>

The environment during the chase is fraught with hazards. **Mandragora** enemies, resembling grass bulbs, pop up and scream, dealing area damage. Burrowing under them allows you to pick them up and throw them. **Hedges** require multiple hits to destroy, and you cannot burrow under them, forcing you to find alternative paths or spend time clearing them.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Crow Town and Environmental Challenges">
After the intense chase sequences, you'll find temporary respite in Crow Town. This area introduces new environmental mechanics, particularly **Lightning Screens**. These screens are periodically struck by lightning. While being caught in the open inflicts moderate damage, standing near **metal swords** will redirect the lightning to them, potentially harming enemies.

<Callout type="info" title="Lightning Mechanics">
Lightning strikes on metal swords can damage you, but more importantly, they can instantly kill enemies. Use this to your advantage to clear paths or take down tougher foes. If you have the Lightning Guard trinket, equip it to make these sections easier.
</Callout>

Crow Town also features various sub-areas with unique challenges. The "Tangled Woods" area, for example, combines wind effects with the Carving Man's pursuit, requiring careful navigation. Here, red Mandragora enemies will leap and explode, adding another layer of danger.

Exploring the town involves clearing out numerous crows and finding hidden passages, such as a house that leads to the **Guardian Casket**, a powerful weapon that auto-equips and allows for enhanced parries and shield bashes.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Carving Man Boss Fight and Tower Minigame">
The culmination of your journey through Septemburg is the boss battle against the **Carving Man**. This fight takes place in a dynamic arena with concrete blocks that the boss can destroy or knock down. Lightning also plays a role, occasionally striking the boss if you're nearby.

### Carving Man Attack Patterns

| Attack Name | Description | Evasion/Counter Strategy |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Forward Punch** | Lunge with a pumpkin-like "hand mouth," 3-4 hits. | Watch mouth direction for warning, dodge or burrow. |
| **Charge** | Straight-up charge across the screen. | Dodge, can clear concrete blocks. |
| **Grab and Throw** | Grabs Mina and throws her, breaking concrete. | Good opportunity to heal after the throw. |
| **Detachable Hands** | Hands detach and chase Mina. | Whip them to destroy them (jump whip is effective). |
| **Electric Rings/Pulses** | Full circle or pulsating spark rings. | Get in the gaps of pulsating rings, move with rotating line. |
| **Rotating Lightning Line** | A line of lightning that rotates across the arena. | Move with the rotation to avoid damage. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Boss Strategy and Sub-Weapons">
The **Beckoning Collar** sub-weapon is excellent for consistent damage, while the **Recall Disc** provides crucial escape options. Focus on landing 3-ish hits after the Carving Man jumps or at the end of his punch combos. Burrowing is vital for avoiding corner traps and the rotating lightning attacks.
</Callout>

After defeating the Carving Man, you'll receive a **Health Rose** for an HP buff and proceed to another **Tower Minigame**. This minigame offers infinite tries and features Mandragora enemies, air paddles, pumpkin shooters, and electricity. Thorne will appear at the top, performing fly-bys with his spear, which you can burrow to ignore. Your goal here is to collect buried bones and reach the top to fix the tower, earning a substantial bone reward.

</Section>

Throughout Septemburg, Mina can acquire various trinkets and sub-weapons that significantly impact gameplay. These items can turn the tide in challenging encounters and provide unique utilities for exploration.

### Key Trinkets and Sub-Weapons

| Item Name | Type | Acquisition | Effect | Strategic Use |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Recall Disc** | Sub-Weapon | Found in Septemburg | Throw, then warp to its location, damaging foes. | Evasion, quick repositioning, surprise attacks. |
| **Beckoning Collar** | Sub-Weapon | Found in Septemburg | Summons a pet that attacks, can be exploded. | Constant damage, area-of-effect burst (at cost of charge). |
| **Guardian Casket** | Weapon | Crow Town house chest | Auto-equips, allows blocking, enhanced parries. | Defensive play, parry-focused combat against aggressive foes. |
| **Lightning Guard** | Trinket | (Acquired earlier) | Reduces damage from lightning. | Essential for Crow Town's Lightning Screens. |
| **Kear** | Currency | Found in hidden areas, chests | Used at Kear Boxes for Bonestone. | Collect for financial boosts, especially 1,000 Bonestone from a Kear Box. |

<Callout type="success" title="Trinket Acquisition from Kids">
Rescuing all three lost kids in Septemburg will grant you a unique Trinket. This is a highly recommended objective to complete during your initial playthrough to avoid purchasing it later at a higher cost.
</Callout>

The choice of sub-weapon can greatly influence your approach to combat. For the Carving Man boss fight, the Beckoning Collar offers a consistent damage source, while the Recall Disc provides unparalleled mobility and evasion, making it easier to avoid his powerful attacks and reposition for your own. Experiment with different combinations to find what suits your playstyle best.
