### Core Gameplay Mechanics

Mina's arsenal includes various weapons, selectable at the game's start, such as a fast whip, a powerful hammer, or swift jewel blades. Combat is four-directional, demanding precise movement and strategic enemy engagement. The "hollowing" ability allows Mina to burrow underground, gaining speed, passing obstacles, and executing extended jumps, which is vital for both combat and intricate platforming.

<Callout type="warning">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Progression on Switch">
Combat in **Mina the Hollower** is a dynamic affair, with each enemy presenting a unique challenge due to the four-directional attack system. The game features a distinctive health system: taking damage leaves a yellow segment on your health bar, which can be replenished by dealing damage to enemies before fully healing with vials. This mechanic adds a strategic layer, encouraging aggressive play to recover health.

### Weapon and Sidearm Variety

The choice of weapon significantly impacts gameplay, with each offering a distinct moveset. Sidearms, powered by collectible jewels, provide special abilities like throwing knives, axes, or a personal favorite, a dodge move that allows Mina to pass through enemies and recover a small amount of health.

| Weapon Type | Primary Attack Style | Special Mechanic | Recommended Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dual Blades** | Very swift, rapid strikes | Quick combos, high mobility | Aggressive, fast-paced |
| **Flail/Mace** | Moderate speed, higher damage | Area-of-effect potential | Balanced, crowd control |
| **Heavy Hammer** | Slow, chargeable attack | Roll while charging, high burst damage | Strategic, patient, heavy hitter |

### Leveling and Bone System

Progression in Mina the Hollower involves collecting bones, which can be used to "bone up" (level up) Mina's attack, defense, or sidearm abilities. A unique risk-reward system governs bones:
- **First death**: Bones remain, respawn at checkpoint.
- **Second death**: All collected bones are lost.
- **Crystallize bones**: Save bones from being lost upon death, but cannot be used for leveling until uncrystallized.

This system, combined with a plethora of trinkets that offer various modifiers, allows for deep character customization to suit individual playstyles.

Mina's hollowing ability is central to both combat and traversal. It allows her to move quickly underground, bypass obstacles, and even sneak past guards. Popping out of the ground can also give her an extra boost for longer jumps, essential for navigating the game's platforming challenges.

The game boasts over 20 distinct boss fights, many of which are multi-stage and require adapting your strategy on the fly. Yacht Club Games has also included optional modifiers in the options menu, ranging from invincibility and enhanced jumps to increased difficulty settings. While some developers might consider these "cheats," they offer players the flexibility to tailor the challenge to their preference.
