<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Optimizing Performance with Proton Experimental">
If you encounter slowdowns, freezes, or crashes with Mina the Hollower on your Linux distribution, switching to Proton Experimental is a highly recommended solution. This compatibility layer allows Windows games to run on Linux with minimal issues.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Steam Play for All Titles">
    Open your Steam client. Go to **Steam > Settings > Steam Play**. Check the box for "Enable Steam Play for all other titles" and select "Proton Experimental" from the dropdown menu. Restart Steam for changes to take effect.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Force Compatibility Tool for Mina the Hollower">
    Navigate to Mina the Hollower in your Steam Library. Right-click on the game, select **Properties > Compatibility**. Check "Force the use of a specific Steam Play compatibility tool" and ensure "Proton Experimental" is selected.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Update Graphics Drivers">
    Ensure your graphics drivers are up-to-date. For Nvidia users, the developer recommends using proprietary Nvidia drivers over Nouveau drivers, which are known to cause performance problems. Check your system's Driver Manager (e.g., on Linux Mint) to verify.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Monitor System Resources">
    While Mina the Hollower is an 8-bit inspired game, unexpected performance drops can occur. Monitor your CPU and GPU temperatures and usage, especially on laptops, to rule out overheating as a cause.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Driver Compatibility">
Using Nouveau drivers for Nvidia GPUs on Linux can lead to severe performance issues and instability. Always prioritize installing the official proprietary Nvidia drivers for the best gaming experience.
</Callout>
