<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and the Hollower Arsenal">

At its heart, Mina is a Hollower—a member of a guild specialized in combat and subterranean movement. Unlike traditional top-down heroes, Mina's primary movement tool is burrowing. This allows her to dive under enemy projectiles, navigate hazardous terrain, and pop up behind foes for critical strikes. This mechanic is essential for survival as you navigate the six major areas of Tenebrous Isle.

Combat is heavily influenced by the *Castlevania* series, featuring a primary weapon (the Morning Star) and a variety of secondary tools found in the environment. These tools are often hidden in candelabras or bushes, requiring players to stay vigilant while exploring.

| Tool Type | Primary Function | Combat Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Morning Star** | Primary Weapon | Long-range whipping and multi-directional strikes |
| **Heavy Shield** | Defensive Tool | Features a parry option and thrust attack |
| **Attack Dog** | Summon Tool | A companion on a leash that attacks nearby threats |
| **Portable Portal** | Utility | Drop a marker and warp back with an explosion |
| **Toxic Clouds** | Area Denial | Creates a lingering hazard for groups of enemies |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression and the Bone Up System">

The progression system in *Mina the Hollower* draws inspiration from *Zelda 2* and modern Soulslikes. The primary currency is **Bones**. These are dropped by enemies and found in chests throughout the world. Unlike traditional RPGs where you level up automatically, Mina uses a "Bone Up" system where you manually invest your currency into specific stats.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Bones">
    Defeat enemies and smash environmental objects to gather currency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Find a Bone Up Point">
    Spend your accumulated Bones to increase Attack, Defense, or Tool Strength.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equip Trinkets">
    Customize Mina's build further by slotting trinkets that provide passive buffs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade Gear">
    Locate specific NPCs like the gorilla shopkeep to gas up your starting weapons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stat Category | Effect on Gameplay | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack** | Increases damage of all weapons | Priority for aggressive players |
| **Defense** | Reduces damage taken from hits | Essential for learning boss patterns |
| **Tool Strength** | Boosts damage and utility of sub-weapons | Great for hybrid builds |
| **Trinket Slots** | Allows more passive items to be equipped | Focus on this for late-game versatility |

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Penalty">
When you die, you drop your Bones. You have one chance to recover them. If you die again before reaching your "corpse," those Bones are lost forever. This is the core Soulslike element of the game.
</Callout>

</Section>
