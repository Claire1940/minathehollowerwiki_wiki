<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics: Burrowing and Combat">
Mina's unique burrowing ability is the cornerstone of its gameplay, serving as a multi-purpose tool for movement, evasion, and even combat. This mechanic allows Mina to zip around the world, dance around enemies underground, and perform longer jumps, adding a layer of strategic depth to every encounter and platforming challenge. Mastering the burrow is crucial for survival and progression.

| Mechanic        | Description                                                                                             | Strategic Use                                                                                             |
| :-------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Burrowing**   | Dive underground for invulnerability, burst of speed, and longer jumps.                                 | Evade attacks, traverse gaps, surprise enemies, reposition in combat.                                     |
| **Weapon Types**| Whip (ranged, quick), Daggers (high DPS), Hammer (slow, powerful), Parry-focused weapons, and more.    | Adapt to enemy types and combat situations; customize playstyle.                                          |
| **Sidearms**    | Consumable secondary attacks (knives, axes, ghost, demon pet, Iron Steed).                              | Provide tactical advantages, deal burst damage, offer unique movement options (Iron Steed for large gaps). |
| **Trinkets**    | Equippable items offering buffs like increased burrow time, wall grabs, revive, DPS multipliers.         | Customize builds for exploration or combat, experiment with synergies.                                    |
| **Bones (XP)**  | Currency for permanent attack/defense upgrades and shop items, lost upon death if not recovered.        | Grind to make challenging sections easier, invest in stats or useful items.                               |

Combat is fast-paced and requires improvisation. Players can choose from a variety of main weapons, each with distinct advantages, and supplement them with Castlevania-inspired sidearms that use up jewels. The game actively encourages players to find their own style and adapt their loadout to the situation at hand.

<Callout type="warning" title="Souls-like Progression">
Similar to Souls games, you'll permanently lose your 'bones' (experience/money) if you die before recovering your spark from the spot where you fell. This adds a significant risk-reward element to exploration and combat.
</Callout>

This design choice, reminiscent of older Zelda titles and even *Dark Souls*, forces players to engage more deeply with the environment, memorizing layouts and discovering secrets through keen observation. The world is richly layered, with hidden passages and alternate routes connecting areas logically.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Difficulty and Accessibility: A Game for Everyone">
Mina the Hollower is undoubtedly a challenging game, often drawing comparisons to *Souls-like* titles due to its unforgiving combat and progression system. Health replenishment is tied to dealing damage, encouraging aggressive play, and the risk of losing 'bones' upon death adds tension. However, Yacht Club Games has implemented one of the most comprehensive accessibility menus seen in modern gaming.

| Difficulty Aspect  | Description                                                                                             | Mitigation/Customization Options                                                                                |
| :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Challenging Combat** | Enemies have unique attacks; bosses are intense and memorable, often changing arena dynamics.           | Experiment with weapons/sidearms/trinkets; grind for bones to level up; utilize accessibility modifiers.         |
| **Risk-Reward Health** | Health vials only refill based on damage dealt; encourages offense.                                     | Strategic use of vials, trinkets that modify healing, or modifiers to adjust health mechanics.                  |
| **Permanent Loss (Bones)** | Lose unspent bones permanently if you can't retrieve your spark after death.                            | Multiple sparks can be gained to prevent loss; modifiers can remove this mechanic entirely.                     |
| **No Map**         | Increases difficulty in navigation and finding secrets.                                                 | Simple map available for purchase; focus on memorable landmarks; modifiers can add checkpoints.                 |
| **Accessibility Modifiers** | Extensive menu to toggle options like damage taken, enemy health, checkpoints, movement physics. | Customize the game to be easier, harder, or simply different, catering to all skill levels and preferences.    |

This modifier menu allows players to adjust nearly every aspect of the game's difficulty, from adding checkpoints and making enemies die in one hit, to increasing damage taken or even experimenting with "Max Barf" mode for a truly disorienting experience. This level of customization ensures that players of all skill levels can find an enjoyable and appropriate challenge.

<Callout type="info">
**Player Agency in Difficulty:** Unlike many challenging games, Mina the Hollower empowers players to define their own experience. Struggling with a boss? Adjust a modifier. Want an even tougher challenge? Turn off checkpoints. The choice is yours.
</Callout>

</Section>
